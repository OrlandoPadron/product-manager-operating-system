# identity/examples/

Real samples of your past work, organized by artifact type. Skills read these to mimic your structure, tone, and level of detail — voice replication's most important fuel.

## Folders

- `messages/` — Slack/Teams messages, emails, async comments
- `prds/` — full PRDs you've written and shipped
- `tickets/` — JIRA tickets in your style
- `announcements/` — release announcements, internal launches
- `release-reviews/` — release-review slide decks (markdown with `---` separators)
- `private/` — **gitignored** — drop unredacted originals here. Skills read them locally; they never get committed or shared.

## How to add examples

Just drop a file into the relevant folder. No registration step. Skills scan dynamically on every invocation.

**Sanitized vs. private**: anything you'd be uncomfortable sharing externally goes in `private/`. Sanitized variants (anonymized names, fake numbers) go in the typed folders and become part of the shareable template.

## How many examples are enough?

3–5 per type is usually enough for the OS to lock onto your voice. More helps for edge cases (e.g., a PRD for a research product vs. a feature launch). Don't worry about getting it perfect on day one — `/setup identity` only needs a handful to bootstrap.
