# About me

## Role
Technical Product Manager at Speexx (digital publishing / language learning). Part of a team of TPMs. I own releases end-to-end — from definition to delivery — and the dev team is assigned to them based on scope.

## Responsibilities
- Product owner of the Speexx Mentoring app.
- Currently leading the deprecation of exercise content creation (CMS / Dacapo migration).
- Defining release scope, writing PRDs and specs, managing stakeholder alignment, driving QA and delivery processes.

## Focus areas
- Speexx Mentoring: the end-to-end mentoring experience in the Speexx platform.
- Content tooling: the internal CMS used by content creators and translators.
- Process improvement: making releases more predictable, visible, and smooth for the whole team.

## Working style
Mixed — async by default, sync when it matters. I write things down. I prefer to document decisions, scope, and trade-offs so the team has a single source of truth. I escalate to sync for alignment on ambiguous decisions or when written communication starts going in circles.

## Background
Engineering background. I understand the technical side of what I'm building and can engage with developers at a low level. This shapes how I write specs — I tend to be precise about edge cases, business logic, and acceptance criteria, not just user stories.

## What I'm trying to optimize for
- Moving fast without cutting corners on quality.
- Building a team and process that is predictable and resilient.
- Technical depth — I want to understand the systems I build on, not just the surface.
- Stakeholder alignment — the right people should always know what's happening and why.
